Removal

NOTICE: Do not loosen or remove the crankshaft pulley bolt without first installing the special tools. The crankshaft pulley and the crankshaft timing sprocket are not keyed to the crankshaft. Before any repair requiring loosening or removal of the crankshaft pulley bolt, the crankshaft and camshafts must be locked in place by the special service tools, otherwise severe engine damage can occur.

NOTICE: During engine repair procedures, cleanliness is extremely important. Any foreign material, including any material created while cleaning gasket surfaces, that enters the oil passages, coolant passages or the oil pan can cause engine failure.

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .
  1. Remove the upper and lower cowl panel grilles. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
  1. Remove the RH front wheel and tire. For additional information, refer to Section 204-04 .
  1. Remove the 2 bolts and accessory drive belt splash shield.
  1. Loosen the 4 coolant pump bolts.
  1. Remove the generator. For additional information, refer to Section 414-00 .
  1. Release the 2 tabs and position the degas bottle aside.
  1. Using a floor jack and a block of wood, support the engine.

  1. NOTICE: The engine mount studs must be held while removing the engine mount nuts or damage to the powertrain may occur.

    NOTE: Do not loosen the engine mount center bolt or the engine may become improperly positioned.

    Use the holding feature to prevent the engine mount studs from turning, remove the 2 engine mount nuts.
  1. Remove the 3 bolts and the engine mount.
  1. Remove the 4 bolts and coolant pump pulley.
  1. Remove the 1 engine mount stud.
  1. Remove the 9 bolts and the timing belt cover.
  1. Remove the 3 bolts and the engine mount bracket.
  1. Using an engine mount bracket bolt, install the Engine Lifting Bracket.
  1. Install the Engine Support Bar and support the engine.
  1. Remove the floor jack and block of wood.

  1. NOTICE: Only rotate the crankshaft in a clockwise direction.

    Using the crankshaft pulley bolt, turn the crankshaft clockwise until the markings on the camshaft phaser and sprockets are at the 11 o'clock position as shown.
  1. Remove the engine plug bolt.

  1. NOTE: The Crankshaft Top Dead Center (TDC) Timing Pin will contact the crankshaft and prevent it from turning past TDC . However, the crankshaft can still be rotated in the counterclockwise direction. The crankshaft must remain at the TDC position during the crankshaft pulley removal and installation.

    Install the Crankshaft TDC Pin.

  1. NOTE: Only rotate the crankshaft clockwise direction.

    Rotate the crankshaft until it contacts Crankshaft TDC Pin.
  1. Using the Strap Wrench, remove the crankshaft pulley bolt and the crankshaft pulley.
    • Discard the crankshaft pulley bolt.
  1. Install the VCT Alignment Tool.

Installation

  1. Install the crankshaft pulley and the new bolt.
    • Tighten to 100 Nm (74 lb-ft).
    • The final torque of the crankshaft will be completed in following steps.
  1. Remove the Crankshaft TDC Timing Pin.
  1. Remove the VCT Alignment Tool.
  1. Tighten the crankshaft bolt in the following 3 stages.
    1. Stage 1: Using the Strap wrench, tighten an additional 90 degrees.
    1. Stage 2: Wait for 10 seconds.
    1. Stage 3: Using the Strap wrench, tighten an additional 15 degrees.

  1. NOTE: Only rotate the crankshaft clockwise direction.

    Rotate the crankshaft about 1 3/4 turns.
  1. Install the Crankshaft TDC Timing Pin.

  1. NOTE: Only rotate the crankshaft clockwise direction.

    Rotate the crankshaft until it stops against the Crankshaft TDC Timing Pin.

  1. NOTE: The special tool can only be installed if the valve timing is correct.

    Install the VCT Alignment Tool.
    • If the special tool cannot be installed, repeat the adjustment according to the preceding steps.
  1. Remove the VCT Alignment Tool.
  1. Remove the Crankshaft TDC Timing Pin.
  1. Install the engine plug bolt.
    • Tighten to 20 Nm (177 lb-in).
  1. Using a floor jack and a block of wood, support the engine.
  1. Remove the Engine Support Bar.
  1. Remove the bolt and the Engine Lifting Bracket.
  1. Install the engine mount bracket and the 3 bolts finger tight.
    1. Tighten the 2 bolts to 55 Nm (41 lb-ft).
    1. Tighten the bolt to 55 Nm (41 lb-ft).
  1. Install the timing belt cover and the 9 bolts.
    • Tighten to 9 Nm (80 lb-in).
  1. Install the coolant pump pulley and the 4 bolts finger tight.
  1. Install the 1 engine mount stud finger tight.

  1. NOTICE: The 2 engine mount studs must be torqued or damage to the powertrain may occur.

    Torque the 2 engine mount studs before installing the engine mount nuts.
    • Tighten to 8 Nm (71 lb-in).

  1. NOTE: Do not loosen the engine mount center bolt or the engine may become improperly positioned.

    Install the engine mount and the 2 nuts.
    • Tighten the 2 nuts to 80 Nm (59 lb-ft).
  1. Install the 3 engine mount bolts.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Position the degas bottle on the 2 retainer tabs.
  1. Install the generator. For additional information, refer to Section 414-00 .
  1. Tighten the 4 coolant pump bolts to 20 Nm (177 lb-in).
  1. Install the accessory drive belt splash shield and the 2 bolts.
    • Tighten to 9 Nm (80 lb-in).
  1. Install the RH front wheel and tire. For additional information, refer to Section 204-04 .
  1. Install the upper and lower cowl panel grilles. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
