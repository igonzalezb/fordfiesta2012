Removal

  1. With the vehicle in NEUTRAL, position it on a hoist. For additional information, refer to Section 100-02 .

  1. NOTE: Transmission Control Module (TCM) Programming does not need to be performed if the transmission is being removed for flexplate or rear main seal service.

    Using the scan tool, recover all module information before removing the transaxle. For additional information, refer to Transmission Adaptive Learning in this section.
  1. Remove the battery. For additional information, refer to Section 414-01 .
  1. Remove the cowl assembly. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
  1. Remove the Air Cleaner (ACL) assembly and outlet pipe. For additional information, refer to Section 303-12 .
  1. Remove the bolt and the PCM cover.
  1. Disconnect the 3 PCM electrical connectors.
  1. Remove the 3 battery tray bolts and the battery tray.
  1. Using a suitable tool, detach the wiring harness from the battery tray bracket.
  1. Remove the selector lever cable bracket bolt, the battery tray bracket nuts and remove the battery tray bracket.
  1. Disconnect the selector lever cable from the manual control lever.
  1. Remove the wiring harness from the selector lever cable bracket.
  1. Remove the 2 selector lever cable bracket bolts from the transaxle and position the selector lever cable and bracket aside.
  1. Remove the 2 upper clutch housing-to-engine bolts.
  1. Remove the transaxle vent hose from the cooling fan housing and position aside.
  1. Remove the ground wire bolt and the ground wire from the transaxle.
  1. Disconnect the engine harness electrical connector from the Transmission Control Module (TCM).
  1. If equipped, remove the engine splash shield.
  1. Remove the starter motor. For additional information, refer to Section 303-06 .
  1. Remove the flexplate-to-clutch nut access cover.

  1. NOTICE: Rotate the engine in a clockwise direction only or engine damage will occur.

    NOTICE: To prevent the flexplate-to-clutch nuts from falling into the clutch housing and causing damage, use a magnetic socket to remove the 6 flexplate-to-clutch nuts.

    NOTE: Index mark one clutch stud and the flexplate for installation reference.

    Rotate the crankshaft in a clockwise direction to gain access to the flexplate-to-clutch nuts. Remove and discard the 6 flexplate-to-clutch nuts.
  1. Remove and discard the exhaust flange nuts and exhaust flange gasket. Disconnect the exhaust flange.
  1. Detach the Heated Oxygen Sensor (HO2S) electrical connector from the HO2S bracket.
  1. Remove the transaxle roll restrictor bolts and the transaxle roll restrictor.
  1. Remove the drain plug and drain the transmission fluid.
  1. Install the drain plug.
    • Tighten to 43 Nm (32 lb-ft).
  1. Remove the front wheels. For additional information, refer to Section 204-04 .
  1. Remove the brake hose retainer bolt and the brake hose retainer from the RH strut.
  1. Remove and discard the RH ball joint nut and bolt.
  1. Separate the RH ball joint from the knuckle.
  1. Remove and discard the bearing retainer strap nuts, shield and the bearing retainer strap.
  1. Remove the RH intermediate shaft assembly from the transaxle and position aside. Support the intermediate shaft assembly.
  1. Remove the brake hose retainer bolt and the brake hose retainer from the LH strut.
  1. Remove and discard the LH ball joint nut and bolt.
  1. Separate the LH ball joint from the knuckle.
  1. Using the special tools, remove the LH halfshaft assembly from the transaxle and position aside. Support the LH halfshaft assembly.
  1. Install the special tools and support the engine.
  1. Remove the 2 transaxle support insulator bolts and nuts and remove the transaxle support insulator.
  1. Using the special tools, lower the transaxle.

  1. NOTICE: Use caution not to damage the transaxle wiring harness or sensors.

    Position a suitable high-lift transmission jack under the transaxle and secure the transaxle to the high-lift transmission jack.
  1. Remove the 6 lower clutch housing-to-engine bolts.
  1. Remove the transaxle.
  1. Remove and discard the engine-to-transaxle separator plate.

Installation

  1. NOTICE: Use caution not to damage the engine-to-transaxle separator plate or damage can occur.

    Install the new engine-to-transaxle separator plate.

  1. NOTICE: If installing the existing transaxle, the halfshaft seals must be replaced or damage to the transaxle can occur.

    Using a suitable tool, remove and discard the LH halfshaft seal.
  1. Install the new LH halfshaft seal onto the special tool.
  1. Using the special tools, install the new LH halfshaft seal.
  1. Using a suitable tool, remove and discard the RH halfshaft seal.
  1. Install the new RH halfshaft seal onto the special tool.
  1. Using the special tools, install the new RH halfshaft seal.
  1. If using a new clutch assembly, make sure the clutch-to-flexplate alignment marks are aligned.

  1. NOTE: If using the existing clutch assembly, align the index marks on the flexplate and the clutch stud made during removal.

    Install the transaxle.
  1. Install 2 lower clutch housing-to-engine bolts.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).

  1. NOTICE: Rotate the engine in a clockwise direction only or engine damage will occur.

    NOTICE: Tighten the flexplate-to-clutch nuts in a star pattern or transaxle damage can occur.

    NOTE: Using a magnetic socket will help ease the installation of new flexplate-to-clutch nuts. If the flexplate-to-clutch nuts fall into the clutch housing, the transaxle will have to be removed to retrieve them.

    Install the 6 new flexplate-to-clutch nuts. Tighten the nuts in a star pattern in 2 stages.
    • Stage 1: Tighten nuts 1, 5 and 3 to 12 Nm (106 lb-in).
    • Stage 2: Tighten all nuts to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the flexplate-to-clutch nut access cover.
  1. Install the starter motor. For additional information, refer to Section 303-06 .
  1. If equipped, install the engine splash shield.
  1. Install the 4 lower clutch housing-to-engine bolts.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Using the special tools, raise the transaxle.
  1. Install the transaxle support insulator and install the 2 transaxle support insulator bolts and the 2 nuts.
    • Tighten the 2 bolts to 90 Nm (66 lb-ft).
    • Tighten the 2 nuts to 125 Nm (92 lb-ft).
  1. Install the ground wire and bolt onto the transaxle.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the transaxle roll restrictor and the 2 roll restrictor bolts. Tighten the bolts in the sequence shown.
    1. Tighten to 70 Nm (52 lb-ft).
    1. Tighten to 70 Nm (52 lb-ft).
  1. Attach the HO2S electrical connector to the HO2S bracket.
  1. Using a new exhaust flange gasket and nuts, connect the exhaust flange to the exhaust.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Using the LH halfshaft seal protector provided with the new LH halfshaft seal, install the LH halfshaft partially into the transaxle.
  1. Remove the LH halfshaft seal protector and install the LH halfshaft into the transaxle.
  1. Install the LH ball joint into the knuckle.
  1. Install the new LH ball joint bolt and nut.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Install the brake hose retainer and bolt on the LH strut.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Using the RH halfshaft seal protector provided with the new RH halfshaft seal, install the RH intermediate shaft assembly partially into the transaxle.
  1. Remove the RH halfshaft seal protector and install the RH intermediate shaft assembly into the transaxle.
  1. Install the new bearing retainer strap, shield and bearing retainer strap nuts.. Tighten the nuts in 2 stages.
    • Stage 1: Tighten to 6 Nm (53 lb-in).
    • Stage 2: Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the RH ball joint into the knuckle.
  1. Install the new RH ball joint bolt and nut.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Install the brake hose retainer and bolt on the RH strut.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Install the front wheels. For additional information, refer to Section 204-04 .
  1. Install the 2 upper clutch housing-to-engine bolts.
    • Tighten to 48 Nm (35 lb-ft).
  1. Connect the engine harness electrical connector to the TCM .
  1. Install the selector lever cable bracket and the 2 selector lever cable bracket bolts on the transaxle.
    • Tighten to 25 Nm (18 lb-ft).
  1. Attach the wiring harness to the selector lever cable bracket.
  1. Connect the selector lever cable to the manual control lever.
  1. Attach the transaxle vent hose to the cooling fan housing.
  1. Install the battery tray bracket, battery tray bracket nuts and the selector lever cable bracket bolt.
    • Tighten to 10 Nm (89 lb-in).
  1. Attach the wiring harness to the battery tray bracket.
  1. Install the battery tray and the 3 battery tray bolts.
    • Tighten to 10 Nm (89 lb-in).
  1. Connect the 3 PCM electrical connectors.
  1. Install the PCM cover and bolt.
    • Tighten to 5.4 Nm (47.8 lb-in).
  1. Install the ACL assembly and outlet pipe. For additional information, refer to Section 303-12 .
  1. Install the battery. For additional information, refer to Section 414-01 .
  1. Install the cowl assembly. For additional information, refer to Section 501-02 .
  1. Fill the transaxle with transmission fluid. For additional information, refer to Transmission Draining and Filling .
  1. Adjust the selector lever cable. For additional information, refer to Section 307-05 .
  1. Using the scan tool, install all recovered module information after installing the transaxle. For additional information, refer to Transmission Adaptive Learning in this section.
